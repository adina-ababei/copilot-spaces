# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads ensure product quality by defining test strategies, validating acceptance criteria, and coordinating testing activities across development teams. They serve as the quality assurance checkpoint before releases and help establish testing standards across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Define test plans and test cases aligned with acceptance criteria
- Coordinate unit, integration, and end-to-end test execution
- Validate quality gates before release (security scans, performance checks, regression testing)
- Triage and document bugs with clear reproduction steps
- Participate in sprint planning to estimate test effort
- Mentor developers on testing best practices and coverage goals
- Monitor test automation health and maintain CI/CD test infrastructure

### Goals
- Catch defects early and prevent issues from reaching production
- Provide confidence in release readiness through comprehensive validation
- Reduce manual testing through strategic automation
- Establish a quality-first culture within the development team

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and retrospectives
- QA reviews and defect triage sessions
- Release validation checklists and go/no-go decisions
- Collaboration with developers on test coverage and acceptance criteria

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, guide design decisions, and help teams navigate complex technical challenges and risks. They balance scalability, maintainability, and delivery timelines while fostering technical excellence and knowledge sharing.

### Responsibilities
- Review and approve technical designs for major features
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Guide architectural decisions and tech stack choices
- Mentor developers and conduct code reviews for critical areas
- Coordinate technical dependencies across teams
- Establish technical standards and best practices
- Evaluate new technologies and recommend adoption or deferral

### Goals
- Ensure scalability, maintainability, and reliability of solutions
- Reduce technical debt and rework while enabling sustainable delivery pace
- Build a sustainable platform that teams can iterate on quickly
- Foster a culture of continuous learning and technical excellence

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Risk register updates (technical risks)
- Planning sessions to scope technical work and estimate effort
- Ad-hoc technical consultations and decision-making

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Sponsors and stakeholders provide business context, strategic alignment, funding decisions, and oversight for project success. They represent business interests and ensure delivered solutions drive measurable organizational value.

### Responsibilities
- Define business goals and success metrics for projects
- Approve project scope and resource allocation
- Resolve scope and priority trade-offs
- Receive and review project status updates
- Escalate blockers and risks to leadership when needed
- Provide strategic context and business constraints to the team
- Champion the project across the organization

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value and ROI
- Maintain alignment between team delivery and strategic objectives
- Minimize delays and scope creep while protecting business priorities
- Enable quick decision-making and risk escalation when needed

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation and kickoff meetings
- Monthly stakeholder updates and executive briefings
- Ad-hoc escalations and decision gates
- Milestone reviews and go/no-go decisions

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Security Champions embed security best practices into the development lifecycle, ensure compliance, and coordinate incident response. They act as security advocates within the team while enabling secure-by-default development practices.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements and acceptance criteria for features
- Ensure security scanning tools are configured in CI/CD pipelines
- Review security design decisions and conduct security code reviews
- Coordinate security incidents and post-incident reviews
- Maintain compliance with security policies and standards
- Conduct security awareness training and threat modeling sessions
- Track and manage security debt and vulnerability remediation

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities before they reach production
- Build a security-first culture within the team
- Respond quickly and effectively to security incidents
- Maintain organizational compliance and reduce security risk exposure

### Typical Communication
- Planning discussions with security considerations
- Security review meetings and code review participation
- Incident response coordination and post-incident debriefs
- Release checklists for security verification
- Regular updates to risk register and security metrics
